Project: Sales Performance E-Commerce

Full Process of Analysis with over 17 millions records: Data Modeling, Processing, Cleansing, Visualizing | Python + SQL + Power BI

Project objectives

The objective of this project is to design and develop a comprehensive Sales Performance Dashboard to monitor and analyze historical beverage sales data from 2017 to 2023.

Within the context of the E-Commerce and Retail industry, the dashboard aims to provide key business stakeholders with timely and actionable insights on:

  • Overall sales trends (volume of bottles sold, total orders)
  • Performance by Vendor, Category, and Store.
  • Sales growth over time (YoY, CAGR)
  • Seasonal patterns and sales fluctuations
  • Operational metrics such as active vendors, store coverage, and order efficiency (AVG bottles/order, AVG bottles/Vendor)

    • This solution enables business leaders, sales managers, and supply chain teams to:
  • Identify high-performing product categories and vendors
  • Detect sales decline or anomalies in specific timeframes or regions
  • Optimize inventory and promotional strategies based on seasonality trends
  • Make data-driven decisions to improve revenue and customer experience in e-commerce operations

    Chapter 2: Data Processing

    I. Issues Identified in Raw Data:

  • Duplicated records: 2.231.371
  • Missing Values in all columns
  • Inconsistencies in columns: Store Number, City Name, Store Name
  • Wrong Data Type: Date - stored as object; Vendor Number - stored as float with missing values in string form ("NOT AVAILABLE")
  • Anomaly / Exception Value: 4.702 records have Bottles Sold < 0 (cause unknown)

    Chapter 3: Data visualization

    1. Define Business Questions & Metrics

  • Identified key business questions related to sales performance, vendor efficiency, category trends, and seasonality.
  • Selected relevant KPIs: total bottles sold, total orders, average bottles per order, YoY growth, CAGR, and vendor/category/store coverage.

    • 2. Draw Dashboard Layout

  • Structured dashboard with a clear top-down information flow: High-level KPIs at the top, Time-based trends in the middle, Performance breakdowns (by vendor, store, category) at the bottom
  • Ensured logical grouping and alignment for readability and usability.

    • 3. Choose Visual Elements

  • Line charts: used to show time-based trends (monthly bottles sold, orders)
  • Bar charts: used for comparing top vendors, categories, and stores
  • Donut chart: used to represent vendor market share
  • YoY growth: visualized using column charts with conditional coloring (positive/negative)

    • 4. Slicers

  • Added slicers (filters) for Year, City, Store, Vendor, and Category to enable dynamic exploration.
  • Ensured all visual elements respond to selected filters for a seamless user experience.

    • 5. Apply Consistent Colour

  • Used a unified color theme (red tones) aligned with product context (beverage/wine)
  • Standardized fonts, spacing, and label formats for visual harmony

    • 6. Highlight Insight & Validate

  • Added data labels and tooltips to ensure clarity
  • Applied data filters for relible insights
  • Cross-checked calculated KPIs and charts against transformed data to ensure accuracy.

    • Key insights:

  • Sales experienced a noticeable decline in 2022, but partially recovered in 2023, indicating early signs of performance stabilization.
  • Sales typically peak around late Q2 and Q4 each year, suggesting strong seasonal demand patterns (possibly tied to holidays and celebrations).
  • A small group of vendors (e.g., Sazerac, Diageo) accounts for a large portion of total bottles sold, indicating a highly concentrated vendor contribution.
  • Certain stores such as Central City Liquor significantly outperform others, which may indicate prime locations or loyal customer base.
  • The American Vodkas category has the highest number of orders and bottles sold, reflecting strong customer preference.
  • The overall CAGR (Compound Annual Growth Rate) remains positive across the 7-year period, showing long-term sales resilience despite short-term dips.
  • Gradual increase in the number of active vendors and product categories over time, suggesting inventory diversification and supplier expansion.
  • The average bottles per order remains relatively stable year-over-year, indicating consistent order sizing behavior.
  • Year-on-year growth is highly variable, suggesting external factors (e.g., policy, economy, market conditions) impact performance.
